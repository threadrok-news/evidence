Full Narration

Ukraine's top military command changed hands Tuesday. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy fired Gen. Oleksandr Syrskyi as commander-in-chief of the armed forces and appointed Mykhailo Drapatyi as his replacement. Syrskyi had held the post since February 2024, when Zelenskyy dismissed his predecessor amid earlier battlefield pressure. This is a significant leadership shift at a critical moment in the war. Pay attention to how Drapatyi's appointment shapes Ukraine's operational posture in the coming weeks. PBS NewsHour confirms the dismissal and the appointment are both official. The transition is immediate.
