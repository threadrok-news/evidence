Full Narration

Wisconsin's Democratic gubernatorial primary shifted sharply this week. Milwaukee County Executive David Crowley is back in the race, and he now has the endorsement of sitting Governor Tony Evers, confirmed by PolitiFact. The reshuffling follows Sara Rodriguez's exit from the race on Friday. Rodriguez's departure was linked to campaign finance errors, which PolitiFact has verified as the stated reason for her withdrawal. Evers threw his support behind Crowley on Saturday, the day after Rodriguez stepped aside. The signal to watch is whether Crowley's entry, backed by the incumbent governor, consolidates the Democratic field or draws new challengers into what remains a competitive battleground primary.
