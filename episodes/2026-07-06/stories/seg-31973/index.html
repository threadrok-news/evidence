Full Narration

Cuba went dark on Monday. An islandwide blackout hit the country as its aging electrical grid failed and fuel reserves continued to fall. This isn't a sudden crisis: Cuba was already struggling to keep the lights on before January, when President Trump imposed an oil blockade on the island. The blockade cut off a key fuel supply to a grid that was already operating near its limits. Dwindling reserves and crumbling infrastructure now compound each other. Pay attention to how long the outage lasts — extended nationwide blackouts carry serious consequences for water, food storage, and hospitals.
