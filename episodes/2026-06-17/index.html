Full Narration

Georgia's Republican legislative leaders have refused to redraw congressional and state legislative districts during the special session Governor Brian Kemp called, which was set to begin Wednesday. The redistricting push came after a U.S. Supreme Court decision weakened federal Voting Rights Act protections for minority voters. Republican lawmakers said they won't consider mid-decade redistricting at this session, effectively shutting down Kemp's proposal before it started. The governor had the authority to call the session but not to compel action once legislators convened. The question now is whether Kemp pursues another path forward or whether the redistricting effort stalls entirely. The Supreme Court ruling that prompted the push remains in effect regardless of what Georgia's legislature decides.
