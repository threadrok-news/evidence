Graham Platner suspended his Maine Senate campaign Wednesday after a woman accused him of sexual assault, with Maine Democrats now facing a scramble to select a replacement nominee.

Full Narration

Maine's Democratic Senate race is over before it began. Graham Platner suspended his campaign Wednesday night after a woman accused him of sexual assault. The allegation, published Monday, includes a claim of removing a condom without consent. Platner denied all of it, calling the allegations false. Snopes.com confirmed the rape allegation was made and that Platner dropped his Maine Senate bid Wednesday night. He had been running for the seat held by Republican Susan Collins. Maine Democrats now need to choose a new nominee. The question is whether the party can identify and vet a replacement candidate quickly enough to mount a credible challenge to Collins in the general election.
