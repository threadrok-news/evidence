Full Narration

The US just signed a nuclear cooperation agreement with Saudi Arabia, and here's the immediate concern: it doesn't include what nonproliferation experts call 'gold standard' safeguards. That means Riyadh isn't prohibited from enriching uranium on its own soil. Trump has claimed the deal bars Saudi enrichment and also requires the kingdom to normalize relations with Israel. Those two claims are contested. The text of the agreement, as corroborated across reporting, does not contain binding enrichment restrictions. The question to track is whether Congress moves to review the deal. Under US law, civilian nuclear agreements with foreign governments require congressional notification. What matters here is the gap between what the administration said this deal contains and what the signed document shows.
