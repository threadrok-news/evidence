Full Narration

About 20,000 people were evacuated from towns and vacation resorts along France's Atlantic coast on Thursday as wildfires swept through the southwest. Spain declared a national emergency in the Madrid region the same day. Combined, roughly 30,000 people have fled fires across both countries. BBC News reports the evacuations hit tourist areas hard, with resorts along the coast emptied during peak summer season. Firefighters across both countries are battling blazes under high heat and dry conditions. Pay attention to the Spain emergency declaration — national emergency status unlocks federal resources and can shift military assets to firefighting operations. The scale across both countries puts this among the larger European wildfire events of recent years.
