Democratic Senate candidate Graham Platner filed to remove himself from Maine's ballot after veterans groups called on him to quit following a sexual misconduct accusation.

Full Narration

Graham Platner, the Democratic candidate in Maine's Senate race, filed paperwork to withdraw from the ballot this week. Veterans groups had publicly called for him to step aside after a woman accused him of removing a condom without her consent. PolitiFact and Snopes both confirmed his status as a Democratic candidate and the seat's current Republican holder, Susan Collins. The withdrawal filing is the hard fact here. Whether it completes the removal process depends on Maine's ballot deadline rules, which have not been detailed in available reporting. The veterans groups' intervention is notable as an organized outside pressure campaign, not just individual voices. Pay attention to whether the Democratic Party moves to name a replacement candidate, and on what timeline. Collins holds the seat. The race now has no confirmed challenger.
