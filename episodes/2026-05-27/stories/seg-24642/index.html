Full Narration

Americans who are exposed to Ebola while abroad will not be allowed to return home — at least not directly. The Trump administration is building a quarantine and treatment facility in Kenya, and the White House has confirmed that exposed Americans will be sent there instead of to the United States. PBS NewsHour reported the plan, which involves blocking Ebola-exposed Americans from re-entering the US and routing them to the Kenya facility during their quarantine period. Construction of that facility is underway. Here's what matters for anyone traveling in Central or East Africa: a confirmed Ebola exposure now triggers a government-directed transfer to Kenya, not a return flight home. The administration has not yet detailed how long quarantine periods would last or what medical standards the facility will meet.
