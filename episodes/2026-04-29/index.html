Jerome Powell will step down as Fed chair in May but stay on the board of governors through 2028, citing Trump administration legal actions as context for the decision.

Full Narration

Jerome Powell is staying on the Federal Reserve board of governors. He's stepping down as Fed chair when his term ends in May, but he'll remain a board member through 2028 — a decision he's framed partly in response to legal actions taken by the Trump administration. PBS NewsHour reports that Kevin Warsh has been confirmed by a Senate committee for the Fed chair role, positioning him as Powell's likely successor at the helm. Powell staying on the board means he retains a vote on monetary policy decisions even after he's no longer chair. Pay attention to whether the administration attempts further legal action to push Powell off the board entirely. That dispute, if it escalates, would land in federal court and directly test the limits of presidential removal power over independent regulators.
