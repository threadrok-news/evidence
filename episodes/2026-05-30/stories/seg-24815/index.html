Full Narration

The Justice Department has asked a federal judge to step aside from a lawsuit against Georgia election officials. The request targets U.S. District Judge Eleanor Ross, an Obama appointee, in the DOJ's case against Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger. The Hill reports the recusal request centers on an event Judge Ross attended honoring Fulton County District Attorney Fani Willis, whose own prosecution of the Georgia election case drew national attention. The test is whether Judge Ross agrees to step down voluntarily or the DOJ pushes the matter further. Some details of the recusal filing have not been independently confirmed across all sourcing.
