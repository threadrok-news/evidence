Democratic nominee Graham Platner formally withdrew from the Maine US Senate race, where he had been set to face Republican incumbent Susan Collins.

Full Narration

The Democratic nominee for Maine's Senate seat has dropped out. Graham Platner, confirmed by Snopes as the Democrat who won the party's nomination, formally withdrew his candidacy, clearing a path for Republicans in one of the most-watched Senate races this cycle. Platner was running against Republican incumbent Susan Collins, confirmed by PolitiFact as Maine's sitting GOP senator. Collins has survived competitive cycles before, but Platner's withdrawal removes the pressure of a fully resourced Democratic challenger. Pay attention to whether Democrats move to recruit a replacement candidate or concede the seat. Maine's Senate race had been flagged as a potential pickup opportunity. That calculus shifts with Platner gone.
