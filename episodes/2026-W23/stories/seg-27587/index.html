An Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo has spread to Uganda, where one death has been confirmed, with no approved treatment for the Bundibugyo strain involved.

Full Narration

The Bundibugyo strain of Ebola, confirmed by Snopes as active in the Democratic Republic of Congo, has now crossed into Uganda, where it has killed at least 1 person. There are no approved drugs that target the Bundibugyo species. That treatment gap is the critical context. Standard Ebola countermeasures developed in response to the more common Zaire strain do not apply here. The WHO revised its suspected case count downward this week, but the geographic spread into a second country shifts the containment calculus entirely. The signal to watch is whether Uganda confirms additional cases beyond the initial fatality, which would determine whether cross-border transmission is isolated or sustained.
