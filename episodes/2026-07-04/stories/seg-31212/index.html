Full Narration

Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, Iran's Supreme Leader, was killed in a U.S.-Israeli airstrike on February 28, in the opening days of the war against Iran. His body is now lying in state at Tehran's Grand Mosalla, where large crowds have gathered for the first day of funeral proceedings. The body will remain at the Grand Mosalla through Monday. The scale of the public mourning in Tehran reflects the weight of this moment for Iran — the country has lost its supreme leader in wartime, a position Khamenei held for decades. What to watch is who steps forward to claim his succession, and how that transition unfolds while Iran remains at war. The next steps in that process are expected in the days following the state funeral.
