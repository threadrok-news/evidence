Supreme Court Halts Alabama Black District Order

0.77 V2×11 V3×2 V5×2
25 articles · SRQ 0.767

The Supreme Court halted a lower court order requiring Alabama to redraw its congressional map to include 2 largely Black districts, restoring the Republican-drawn lines for now.

Full Narration

Alabama's congressional map is back in Republican hands. The Supreme Court on Monday wiped out a lower court order that had required Alabama to draw 2 largely Black congressional districts under the Voting Rights Act. The Court issued an order halting that prior ruling, effectively restoring the Republican-drawn map while legal proceedings continue. This follows a broader ruling from the Court that narrowed how the Voting Rights Act applies to congressional redistricting. The test is whether the underlying legal challenge to Alabama's map proceeds or stalls entirely. The districts in question represent significant Black voting population in the state. For voters in those districts, the map they'll use in the next election is now an open question pending further court action.
