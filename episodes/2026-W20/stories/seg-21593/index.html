Full Narration

Sam Altman is on record now. The OpenAI chief executive said publicly this week that Elon Musk sought control of the company before their falling-out, a claim that reframes years of public dispute between two of the most prominent figures in AI development. Altman's account, reported by NPR and The Guardian, characterizes Musk's early involvement as an attempt to take a controlling stake, not simply to fund the mission. Musk has not issued a direct on-record rebuttal to this specific framing this week. The two sides have issued conflicting accounts of OpenAI's founding dynamics for months, with active litigation in the background. The question is whether this public statement shifts any ground in the ongoing legal proceedings between Musk's entities and OpenAI.
