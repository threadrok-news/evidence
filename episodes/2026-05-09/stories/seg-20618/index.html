Virginia's Supreme Court struck down a voter-approved redistricting plan 4-3, reverting the state to existing congressional maps while Democrats appeal.

Full Narration

Virginia's congressional maps are back to square one. The state Supreme Court struck down a voter-approved redistricting plan by a 4-3 margin, throwing out a referendum result that Democrats had won. NPR reports the court ruled the plan violated constitutional requirements. Democrats are now appealing. The ruling means Virginia's current congressional district lines remain in place while litigation continues. Here's the context: the redistricting referendum was designed to give voters, rather than the legislature, control over how district boundaries are drawn. The 4-3 split on the court signals this is not a settled question. The appeal will determine whether the voter-approved maps can be reinstated before the next election cycle.
