Full Narration

Spain is preparing evacuation protocols as a cruise ship carrying confirmed hantavirus cases heads toward the Canary Islands. The WHO has confirmed 6 cases linked to the vessel. Hantavirus is a rare but serious illness — transmission typically requires close contact with infected rodents or their droppings. Spanish authorities are coordinating with port and health officials ahead of the ship's arrival. What to watch is whether additional cases emerge among passengers or crew before the vessel docks. The 6 confirmed cases represent what the UN health agency has publicly verified so far. Hantavirus does not spread person-to-person, which limits broader outbreak risk. But the concentration of cases aboard a closed vessel complicates containment. Authorities have not confirmed a timeline for evacuations or how many passengers are aboard.
