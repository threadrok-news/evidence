Michele Spagnuolo, a Google engineer, faces insider trading charges after allegedly using access to the company's Year in Search data to place over $2.7 million in winning bets on Polymarket.

Full Narration

$1.2 million. That's what a Google engineer allegedly made betting on his own company's internal data. Michele Spagnuolo has been charged with insider trading after placing wagers on Polymarket tied to Google's 2025 Year in Search campaign, which he had access to before it went public. According to The Hill, Spagnuolo risked over $2.7 million total across those wagers. The charge is insider trading, meaning prosecutors allege he used non-public information to place bets he knew would pay off. Polymarket is a prediction market platform where users bet on real-world outcomes. Pay attention to how this case develops, because it's the first high-profile insider trading charge tied specifically to a prediction market. The legal question of whether prediction market bets fall under existing securities law hasn't been tested at this level before.
