Virginia Democrats asked the U.S. Supreme Court to revive a voter-approved congressional map after Virginia's state supreme court struck it down last week.

Full Narration

Virginia Democrats went to the U.S. Supreme Court on Monday asking justices to restore a congressional map that voters approved at the ballot box. Virginia's state supreme court threw out that map just last week. The question now is whether the federal court will intervene before the next election cycle locks in different district lines. The map in question was put before voters directly and passed. That voter approval is the core of the Democrats' argument: a court overturning a voter-ratified map raises questions about the weight of direct democratic decisions versus judicial review at the state level. Congressional maps determine which party holds seats. A redrawn map before the next cycle could shift competitive districts. The Supreme Court hasn't yet indicated whether it will take up the emergency request.
