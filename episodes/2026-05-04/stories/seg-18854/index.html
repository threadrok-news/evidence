Full Narration

Ships are stuck in the Strait of Hormuz right now, and the United States is moving to get them out. President Trump announced a naval escort mission to guide stranded vessels through the strait, where 2 ships have already reported attacks. The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most critical shipping chokepoints. Any disruption there ripples into global oil supply almost immediately. Here's what matters: this is an active naval commitment, not a diplomatic statement. NPR reports the mission is framed as a protective escort operation. The administration has not specified how many naval assets are involved or how long the mission will run.
